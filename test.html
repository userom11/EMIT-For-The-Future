EMIT - A new sensor to fill data gaps in our observation
of the planet.


The Earth Surface Mineral Dust Source Investigation (EMIT) is a hyperspatial visible/short-wave sensor that can observe multiple wave lengths in 1 data set. Its was launched to the space station on 14th of july 2022, and development is fast and there is already an application for it. It has many use cases in disaster management, and scientific observation. As of date the use cases of EMIT are:

Greenhouse gas mapping Methane and carbon dioxide are the two dominant greenhouse gasses, and EMIT will share research grade methane and CO2 emissions detections to improve our understanding of anthropogenic emissions and to mitigate those emissions.

Snow properties and water resources Snow and ice are important for climate and water resources, and VSWIR imaging spectroscopy is a powerful tool for studying them.

EMIT Mineral Detection


EMIT can identify emmision different mineral including methane and carbon dioxyde alowing it to locte greenhouse emmisions. Identifying greehouse gas emmision sources offers the potential to improve our understanding of anthropogenic emissions and to mitigate those emissions.

Snow and ice are important for our planet because they reflect sunlight back into space. As snow and ice melt, the Earth becomes darker and absorbs more sunlight, which causes the planet to warm. VSWIR imaging spectroscopy is a powerful tool that can be used to study snow and ice in detail. This information can be used to improve climate models and to develop strategies for adapting to climate change.
